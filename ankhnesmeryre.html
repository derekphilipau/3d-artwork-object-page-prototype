Statue of Queen Ankhnes-meryre II and Her Son, Pepy II

EGYPTIAN, CLASSICAL, ANCIENT NEAR EASTERN ART
On View: Old Kingdom to 18th Dynasty, Egyptian Galleries, 3rd Floor

A masterpiece of Middle Kingdom royal sculpture, this statue of the Twelfth Dynasty Statue of Queen Ankhnes-meryre II and Her Son, Pepy II encapsulates one basic theme of this installation: the interaction between permanence and change. By the time an artist carved this piece, the seated pose had been part of Egyptian tradition for more than eight hundred years; most of the elements of the costume had been around even longer. However, the style of carving—note Senwosret’s expressive face—had been in vogue for less than a generation.
